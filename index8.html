TAG

[tæg]

noun

    1. a label attached to someone or something for the purpose of identification or to give other information "he gave his pet a collar with a metal name tag"
    • synonyms: label, ticket, badge, mark, market, tab, tally, sticker, docket, stub, chit, chitt, counterfoi, flag, stamp "a price tag"
    1. a small piece or part thet is attached to a main body

verb

    1. attach a label to:
    • "mother suspected that their babies had been wrongly tagged during an alarm at the hospital"
    • synonyms: label, attach tags to, put a label on, mark, ticket, eamark, identify, docket, flag, indicate
    • "the bottles were tagged with colour-coded labels"
    1. add to something, especially as an afterthought or with no real connection
    • "she meant to tag her question or at the end of her remarks"
    • synonyms:add, tack, join, attach, append, affix, annex
    • "their heads were so large that the rest of their bodies seemed tagged on as an afterthought"
